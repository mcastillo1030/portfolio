The Golden Compass

Summary

Lyra is rushing to the cold, far North, where witch clans and armored bears rule. North, where the Gobblers take the children they steal--including her friend Roger. North, where her fearsome uncle Lord Asriel is trying to build a bridge to a parallel world.

Can one small girl make a difference in such great and terrible endeavors? This is Lyra: a savage, a schemer, a liar, and as fierce and true a champion as Roger or Lord Asriel could want.

But what Lyra doesn't know is that to help one of them will be to betray the other. And that she alone is destined to win, or to lose, this more-than-mortal battle.

A masterwork of storytelling and suspense, Philip Pullman's award-winning The Golden Compass is critically acclaimed and hailed as a modern fantasy classic. It is the first in the His Dark Materials series, which continues with The Subtle Knife and The Amber Spyglass.

About the Author

Sir Philip Pullman is an English novelist. He is the author of several best-selling books, including the His Dark Materials trilogy.

Born in Norwich, England, he grew up in Llanbedr in Ardudwy, North Wales. He graduated from Exeter College in Oxford with a degree in English. He married Judith Speller in 1970 and briefly taught children aged 9 to 13 at Bishop Kirk Middle School in Summertown, North Oxford.

Publication of his second children's book, The Ruby in the Smoke in 1986 prompted him to stop teaching, to which he later returned as a part-time professor at Westminster College in Oxford.

He began writing His Dark Materials in 1993, with the first installment being published in 1995 as Northern Lights in the UK. Pullman won both the Annual Carnegie Medal and the Guardian Children's Fiction Prize for the first installment, with high critical praise following for the later two books. In the US, The Golden Compass won many more accolades, which quickly garnered him recognition as one of this generation's great children's literature authors.
